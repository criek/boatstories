// voyage summary

MV Stellarfjord departed Immingham on 14 Nov 2024, bound for Bergen via the central North Sea corridor. The vessel tracked north-northwest across the Dogger Bank before turning onto a northerly heading through the Norwegian Trench. Transit duration: 61 hours. Four subsea cable crossings were logged en route.

// cable crossing events

At 0312 UTC, [nemo-crossing] was recorded at 54.8°N as the vessel passed over the NEMO interconnector linking the UK and Belgium. Crossing speed was reduced to 6 knots per standing cable protection orders. No anomalies recorded.

By mid-morning, [nord-link-approach] brought the vessel over the NordLink HVDC cable running between Norway and Germany. The crossing occurred in deteriorating weather — wind force 7, 3.5m swell — and was logged as routine. Cable protection zone observed without incident.

The most operationally complex crossing was [viking-link-transit], where the vessel tracked parallel to the Viking Link corridor for approximately 14 nautical miles before the designated crossing point. Proximity to the cable route triggered an automatic AIS alert. Bridge watch was doubled and anchor was secured for the duration.

[norwegian-trench-entry] marks the vessel's descent into the Norwegian Trench, crossing a cluster of Norwegian Petroleum Directorate-registered pipeline routes. Depth increases sharply to over 700m. Reduced speed maintained until clear of the restricted zone.

// master's remarks

All four crossing events — [nemo-crossing], [nord-link-approach], [viking-link-transit], and [norwegian-trench-entry] — were completed in accordance with applicable cable protection protocols. No emergency procedures were required. Vessel arrived Bergen 16 Nov 2024 at 1634 UTC.
